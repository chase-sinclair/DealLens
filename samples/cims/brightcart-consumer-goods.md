# BrightCart Consumer Goods - Confidential Information Memorandum

## Transaction Overview

Northlake Securities is marketing BrightCart Consumer Goods, a family-owned specialty retail and ecommerce brand. The shareholders are exploring a sale to fund succession planning and inventory expansion.

The company is presented as an omnichannel consumer platform, but financial performance has been pressured by lower wholesale demand and rising customer acquisition costs.

## Business Model

BrightCart sells premium household products through owned ecommerce, two retail locations, and wholesale relationships with boutique retailers. Revenue is primarily transactional product sales, with limited subscription or recurring revenue.

## Customer And Channel Data

The CIM does not disclose top customer concentration or wholesale channel concentration. Management notes that retail traffic declined during FY2025 and ecommerce customer acquisition costs increased materially.

## Investment Highlights

- Recognized niche consumer brand.
- Owned ecommerce channel provides customer data.
- Potential to improve merchandising and conversion.

## Growth Opportunities

- Improve ecommerce conversion rate.
- Rationalize retail footprint.
- Introduce bundles and replenishment-based product programs.
- Improve inventory planning.

## Key Risks

- Declining revenue.
- EBITDA margin below the target thesis.
- Limited recurring revenue.
- Customer and channel concentration not disclosed.
- Consumer demand appears discretionary and marketing-cost sensitive.

## Preliminary Diligence Requests

- Channel-level profitability.
- Inventory aging detail.
- Customer acquisition cost and repeat purchase behavior.
- Wholesale customer concentration.
